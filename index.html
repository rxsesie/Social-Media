Document One

Listing Agreement

This is your contract with the broker. It gives your agent the legal right to market and sell your home on your behalf. Everything else in your listing flows from this document.

Parties & Property

Para. 1–2

Names you (Seller) and The Agency Team, LLC (Broker). Your legal contract is with the brokerage — not just your individual agent. The property section defines exactly what is being sold: the land, everything permanently attached (appliances, fans, fixtures, HVAC), and accessories that come with the home.

ImportantIf you want to take something with you — a chandelier, a mounted TV, a garden sculpture — it must be listed as an Exclusion before you sign. Anything not excluded is included in the sale.

Listing Price

Para. 3

The price your agent will advertise the home at. This is your starting point — you always have final say on whether to accept any offer. You're agreeing to sell at this price or any price acceptable to you.

Term

Para. 4

The listing is active from March 24, 2026 through September 24, 2026 — six months. During this period, you cannot sign with another broker without written approval. If you already have a binding sale contract when this begins, the listing is void automatically.

Commission

Para. 5

You agree to pay 6% of the sale price at closing. The title company deducts this automatically from your proceeds — you don't write a separate check. This total is shared between your listing broker and the buyer's broker per their agreement.

Protection PeriodAfter the listing expires, there's a 21-day window. If someone who toured the home during the listing buys it within those 21 days, commission is still owed. Your agent will send you a list of those buyers within 10 days of expiration.

MLS & Marketing

Para. 6

Your home will be filed with the MLS within 5 days of the listing start date — putting it on Zillow, Realtor.com, and hundreds of other sites. You grant the broker a permanent license to use all photos and content you provide, even after the listing ends.

Access & Keybox

Para. 7

You're authorizing agents, inspectors, and appraisers to enter at reasonable times. A keybox is authorized for this listing — showings are managed through Showing Smart. The broker is not liable for injury or theft during access.

Before Your First ShowingRemove medications, jewelry, firearms, and personal financial documents. Once the keybox is on, strangers will walk through your home.

Intermediary

Para. 9

Selected for this listing. If a buyer also represented by your broker makes an offer, the broker becomes a neutral intermediary — they can no longer advise either side strategically. They facilitate the transaction but cannot take your side in negotiation.

Your Obligations

Para. 13

Cooperate with showings, keep disclosures updated, and — critically — refer all buyer inquiries to your agent. Do not negotiate directly with anyone, even a neighbor who expresses interest. Always say: "Please contact my agent."

Document Two

Seller's Disclosure Notice

Texas law requires you to disclose everything you know about your property's condition. This isn't a warranty — it's a record of your honest knowledge on the date you sign it.

Section 1
What the Home Has

Features Checklist

A checklist of items in the home — ceiling fans, AC, heat, pool, garage, water heater, and more. Mark Y / N / Unknown for each. At the end, you answer whether any items are not working or need repair. Be specific if yes.

NoteChecking "Yes" that an item exists does not mean it conveys. The sales contract determines what transfers — this form is only about condition.

Section 2
Known Defects

Structural & Mechanical

Are you aware of defects in: ceilings, doors, electrical, exterior walls, floors, foundation, plumbing, roof, windows? Mark honestly. Foundation and roof are the two items buyers scrutinize most in this region.

Section 3
Special Conditions

Hazards & History

Covers: aluminum wiring, asbestos, diseased trees, termites (active or prior), previous fires, previous foundation/roof repairs, lead paint, wood rot, soil movement, hazardous waste, and more.

Required DisclosurePrior termite treatment, previous flooding, or repaired foundation damage must be disclosed even if fully remediated. Hiding known defects can result in post-closing lawsuits and fraud claims.

Sections 5–7
Flood History

Critical in Liberty County

You must disclose: flood zone designation (100-year / 500-year / floodway), any previous flooding from natural events or reservoir releases, water penetration into structures, any flood insurance claims ever filed, and any FEMA or SBA disaster assistance received.

Non-NegotiableIf this property experienced flooding during any storm event — Harvey, the 2019 floods, or any other — you must disclose it regardless of whether you repaired everything. Buyers in this area will look for this specifically.

Section 8
Other Disclosures

HOA, Permits, Legal

Unpermitted work or code violations, HOA membership details and unpaid dues, deed restriction violations, pending lawsuits, health or safety hazards, mold remediation, propane service area, subsidence district.

Unpermitted WorkIf you added a room, deck, or structure without pulling permits, disclose it here. Inspectors find these — it's better for buyers to know upfront.

Sections 9–13
Final Items

Inspections, Taxes, Smoke

Inspections: Any professional inspection reports from the last 4 years must be attached and disclosed.
Tax exemptions: Your homestead or senior exemption will not transfer — the buyer's taxes may be significantly higher than yours. They should verify independently.
Smoke detectors: Confirm working detectors are installed per Texas law (Chapter 766).

Document Three

HOA Addendum

Encino Estates POA · 281-354-1299. Because this property has a mandatory homeowners association, this addendum governs how HOA information is handled during the sale.

Subdivision Information

Section A

"Subdivision Information" means the HOA's deed restrictions, bylaws, rules, and a Resale Certificate. The Resale Certificate confirms current dues, any money owed by the seller, and the HOA's financial standing.

For this transaction, Option 4 is selected — the buyer has waived the right to receive the full HOA packet. The HOA is still authorized to release information if requested by the buyer or title company.

Material Changes

Section B

If you learn of any significant HOA change — a special assessment, fee increase, or major rule change — before closing, you must notify the buyer. This gives them the right to terminate if the change is materially adverse.

Transfer Fees

Section C · $250 Cap

The buyer pays all HOA transfer fees, deposits, and charges up to $250. Any amount above $250 is paid by you, the seller. Regular ongoing dues are prorated separately at closing based on the closing date.

Document Four

Propane Gas Addendum

A required legal notice disclosing that this property may be located in a propane gas system service area instead of municipal natural gas.

What This Means

TREC No. 47-0

This area may be served by a private propane distribution company rather than city gas lines. This is common in rural Liberty County. The buyer is being informed that: there may be setup costs or deposits to establish propane service, there may be a time period to construct infrastructure before service begins, and they should contact the propane distributor to understand rates and timing before closing.

Texas law (§141.010, Utilities Code) requires this notice plus a copy of any recorded notice the propane company has filed with the county clerk.

Document Five

On-Site Sewer Facility

This property is not connected to municipal sewer. This notice informs the buyer about the septic system they will inherit — what type it is, its condition, and their responsibilities.

System Description

Section A

Disclose the type of system (Septic Tank or Aerobic Treatment), the type of distribution system and drain field location, who installed it, and approximately how old it is. Fill in what you know — mark Unknown for what you don't.

Aerobic Systems Require a Maintenance ContractIf this is an aerobic treatment system, a licensed maintenance contract must be active at all times. This contract must transfer to the buyer at closing or the buyer must establish a new one.

Maintenance History

Section B

Disclose: any active maintenance contract and its expiration, when the tank was last pumped, any known defects or malfunctions, and whether warranty documentation is available. A septic tank should generally be pumped every 3–5 years.

Permit Transfer

Section C

The notice states in bold: "It may be necessary for a buyer to have the permit to operate an on-site sewer facility transferred to the buyer." Check with Liberty County about their specific transfer requirements — this step is often overlooked and can delay closing.

Document Six

Wire Fraud Warning

The single most important practical warning in your entire document package. Wire fraud targeting real estate closings is a multi-billion dollar crime — and it targets sellers and buyers exactly like you.

How It Happens

Know the Attack

Criminals monitor real estate email threads — often through a hacked agent or title company account. Just before closing, they send an email that looks exactly like the title company, with updated wiring instructions. The victim wires their money. It's gone in minutes, often moved internationally and irretrievable.

The One Rule That Protects YouIf you receive ANY email, text, or message with wiring instructions — stop. Do not wire anything. Call your title company using a phone number you already had saved, not one found in the message. Verify every wiring instruction by voice, every time.

The Agency Team will never send wiring instructions by email, text, or social media. If you receive such a message appearing to come from us, it is fraud.

Information About Brokerage Services

Texas law requires agents to give you this form before providing any brokerage services. It explains the different ways an agent can legally represent a party in a transaction.

Broker vs. Agent

Roles Defined

A Broker holds the license and bears full legal responsibility for all transactions — that's The Agency Team, LLC. A Sales Agent (Yasema Robles) works under the broker's authority. Your contract is with the brokerage; your day-to-day contact is your agent.

Your Agent's Minimum Legal Duties

Required by Texas Law

Your agent must by law: put your interests above their own, inform you of all material information, present all offers and counter-offers, and treat all parties honestly and fairly. These are not optional — they are enforceable obligations.

Document Eight

Consumer Notice

A Texas REALTORS® educational notice covering important topics every seller and buyer should understand before completing a transaction.

Appraisal ≠ Inspection

Key Distinction

An appraisal determines your home's value for the lender. An inspection determines the physical condition of the home for the buyer. Two different professionals, two different purposes. Agents are neither — they cannot opine on property condition.

Flood & Flood Insurance

Liberty County Context

REALTORS® encourage buyers to purchase flood insurance even if the property is not in an official flood zone. In Liberty County, even properties outside flood zones were impacted by major storms. The landlord's or seller's prior insurance never covers the new buyer — they must obtain their own.

Tax Prorations & Exemptions

Closing Math

Taxes are prorated at closing — you pay your share through closing day, the buyer pays theirs after. Your homestead or senior exemptions will not transfer. The buyer's tax bill will likely be higher than what you've been paying. Always advise buyers to check the unexempted rate.

Option Period

The Buyer's Exit Window

Most contracts include an option period (typically 7–10 days) during which the buyer can back out for any reason and receive their earnest money back. They pay a small, non-refundable option fee for this right. The option period is not extended by ongoing repair negotiations — extensions must be negotiated separately, in writing, with an additional fee.

MLS Data Accuracy

Buyer Verification

Square footage, tax data, school boundaries, and other information in the MLS comes from third-party sources like appraisal districts. Agents do not verify this data. Buyers and sellers should independently confirm any information they are relying on — especially square footage if it affects the sale price.
